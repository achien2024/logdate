Purpose:

I started to journal when I had a thought too strong to burden. Writing it down or typing it in my phone note felt like relief. Relinquishing something that bothered me. It was like preserving a part of me that I wanted to revisit, because these thoughts highlighted a product. My mentality, cultural norms and shifts, personal and shared experiences, and observations I saw. It was always questions I felt I shouldn’t be asking. Be it, it made others uncomfortable, others didn’t want to think about it, or just plainly others thought I was wrong or weird. But these journal entries, preserved moments of life, never really stayed in a stamp of time. Rather, I always found myself re-visiting the same thoughts after finding insight from other friends, media I consumed, or some experienced just opened the gate again and I would rush back and write.

Once I matured a bit and just learned that there is no shame in pondering and wondering, asking the uncomfortable and addressing elephants in the room, I wanted to release them into the public. Beyond my two friends that I share them with because after all, statistically speaking, there are others who wonder the same.

Logdate isn’t meant to persuade, but rather open. To spread amongst friends, to observe society and ponder. For conversations and thoughts that linger after the party withdraws. In statistics, the first thing you learn is that correlation does not mean causation. And to find the root of a cause, you need to ask why at least six times. I hope these collection of thoughts, journals, blogs, or whatever you’d think best describes them, makes you ask. To ask again, and again, until you find an answer that fits. Could be political, apolitical, philosophical, ignorant, the opinion is yours to share or withhold. As long you first ask.
